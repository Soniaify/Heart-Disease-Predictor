Welcome!

This little app is like a smart doctor friend. It helps you check if someone might have heart disease — just by answering a few health questions.

But don’t worry! You don’t need to be a doctor, and you don’t need to understand big medical words. Just follow the steps and answer the questions as best as you can.

This app uses something called machine learning, which means it has looked at lots and lots of health records before, and it has learned how to spot signs of heart problems.

What You Need to Do

Using the app is very easy — just like filling a form:

  1. Open the App: Run the app, and it will open in your web browser (like Chrome or Firefox).
  2. Fill Out the Health Form: You’ll answer questions like:
  • Age: How old are you?
  • Sex: Are you Male or Female?
  • Chest Pain Type: Do you feel chest pain?
  • Resting Blood Pressure: What is your blood pressure?
  • Cholesterol: How much fat is in your blood?
  • Fasting Blood Sugar: Is your blood sugar more than 120 before eating? (Yes or No)
  • Resting ECG Result: What does your heart test look like when you’re not moving? (Normal or ST-T wave abnormality)
  • Max Heart Rate Achieved: How fast does your heart beat when you exercise a lot?
  • Exercise Induced Angina: Do you get chest pain when you exercise? (Yes or No)
  • Oldpeak (ST Depression): How much does your heart signal drop when under stress?
  • Slope of ST Segment: What shape does your heart signal take? (Upsloping, Flat, Downsloping)
  • Major Vessels Colored by Fluoroscopy: How many big heart blood tubes are blocked? (0 to 3)
  • Thalassemia: What kind of red blood cell condition do you have? (Normal, Fixed Defect, Reversible Defect)
  1. Click "Predict": The app will think for a second and give you a result like:
  • You don’t have heart disease
  • Your heart disease is mild
  • Your heart disease is moderate
  • Your heart disease is severe
  • Your heart disease is very severe

What Is It Doing Behind the Scenes?

When you click “Predict,” the app checks your answers and compares them to thousands of examples it has learned from.

It’s like a smart memory that helps decide what your results might be.

A Final Note from the App

I’m not a real doctor — just a smart helper. If I say you might have heart disease, don’t panic! Go see a real doctor for proper care and advice.
